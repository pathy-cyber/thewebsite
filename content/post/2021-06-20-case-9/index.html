Mediastinal tumor in a 60 year old man


Immunohistochemistry


CK19

CD20

CD1a

What’s your diagnosis


Micronodular Thymoma with lymphoid stroma

Notes


Definion:
Rare subtype of thymoma characterised by numerous lymph follicles

Epidemiology and clinical features:
1-5% of all thymomas.
Age: 41 to 83 years (mean: 63.8 years).
No myastenia gravis.
Up to 30% have associated type A thymoma.

Microscopy:
Small nodules of tumour cells separated by abundant lymphoid stroma, which usually contains lymphoid follicles with or without germinal centres.
Epithelial islands can be highlighted by CK19. Lymph follicales can be highlighted with CD20. Often contain numerous Langerhans cells.

Therapy and Prognosis:
There have been no reports of recurrences, distant metastases, or tumour-related deaths after surgical resection (Only thymoma that has no “/3” ICD-O code).

Reference:
WHO Classification 2021.
