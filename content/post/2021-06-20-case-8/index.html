Desmoid Fibromatosis

Notes


Definion:
Locally aggressive fibroblastic / myofibroblastic tumor without metastatic potential

Epidemiology and Localization:
Mean age: 36 - 42 years

Microscopy:
Bland spindle cells, typically with low mitotic activity.
Poorly defined.
Extravasated erythrocytes common.
May have myxoid, oedematous, or highly collagenous areas.

Immunohistochemistry and molecular pathology:
90% CTNNB1 (exon 3 most common) and APC mutations .

Therapy and Prognosis:
Recurrence in 20-30%
May undergo spontaneous regression.
May be managed conservatively, with surgery, radio- or chemotherapy. Surgery may induce growth, if not completely removed.
