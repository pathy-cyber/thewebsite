Cardial tumor in a 40 year old woman


What’s your diagnosis


Cardiac myxoma

Notes


Definion: Cardiac myxoma is a benign neoplasm consisting of stellate, ovoid, or plump spindle cells within a vascular myxoid matrix.

Epidemiology and Localization:
Left atrium in 80-90%
Non-left atrial and multifocal Myxomas should raise suspicion for Carney Complex (10%; LAMB syndrome (lentigines, atrial myxoma, mucocutaneous myxoma, blue naevus); NAME syndrome (naevi, atrial myxoma, skin myxoma, ephelides)).
May present with paraneoplastic syndromes.
F:M = 2:1
All ages affected, most common 40-70.


Microscopy:
Characterized by so called myxoma cell (or lepidic cell), which is frequenly located around vessels.
Intratumoural elastic fibres (Gamna–Gandy bodies).
Hemorrhage is common.
Extramedullary haematopoiesis in 7%.
If calcifications extensive also called lithomyxoma.

Therapy and Prognosis:
Recurrence in <5% non-Carney Complex and 10-20% in Carney Complex
