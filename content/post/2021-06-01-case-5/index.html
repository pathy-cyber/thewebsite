Rib mass in a 40 year old woman


What’s your diagnosis


Fibrous dysplasia

Notes


Definition: Benign fibroosseous lesion; mono- and polyostotic form

Epidemiology:
Females and Males equally affected.

Microscopy:
Bland fibroblast-like spindle cells.
Multinucleated giant cells may be present (secondary AKZ-like changes).
Myxomatous areas may be present.
Irregular woven bone often described as forming pattern resembling chinese characters
Osteoblastic rimming of the bony trabeculae is often absent.

Molecular pathology:
GNAS1 (exon 8 and 9) in 45–93% of cases.

Syndroms:McCune-Albright syndrome (endocrine abnormalities, café au lait spots) and Mazabraud syndrome (soft tissue myxomas)
